## HIPAA Applicability to SIST™

SIST™ operates under a local-first, operator-controlled architecture with no cloud storage, no client accounts, and no remote transmission of case data. This section clarifies how HIPAA applies — and where it does not — given this architecture.

---

## Does SIST™ Create HIPAA Obligations?

HIPAA applies to **covered entities** (healthcare providers, insurers, clearinghouses) and their **business associates** (vendors who receive Protected Health Information on their behalf).

Under SIST™'s operating model:

- SIST™ does not receive PHI on behalf of a covered entity through a cloud or network transmission
- Client health information referenced in legal cases (e.g., injury details in a personal injury narrative) is processed locally by the operator, not transmitted to SIST™ infrastructure
- The operator handles any health-related case information as a **confidential legal matter**, not as a HIPAA-covered transaction

If a client's case involves medical records or PHI and the operator is working in a capacity that triggers HIPAA covered entity or business associate status, the operator should consult legal counsel regarding applicable obligations.

---

## Operator Commitment Regardless of HIPAA Status

Whether or not HIPAA formally applies, the operator commits to:

- Treating all health-related client information as strictly confidential
- Not disclosing health information to third parties without client consent or legal compulsion
- Not using health information for any purpose other than the client's legal matter
- Not storing health information in any remote or third-party system

---

## If a BAA Is Required

If a client or referring firm determines that a Business Associate Agreement is legally required for their engagement with SIST™, the operator will execute an appropriate BAA. Contact **legal@sist.app** to initiate.
