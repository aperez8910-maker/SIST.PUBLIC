## I. The Perez Method

I am Alexander Emilio Perez. I was born October 5, 1989, in Chicago Heights, Illinois. I was crowned King Sharky in the Silent Latin Kings at 16. I was shot at, I shot back, I ran, I was caught, I did time in TDCJ — Holiday, Gist, Jester III, Walls. I became a plumber. I built Capital Mota. I became a father. I faced a felony gun charge in Bexar County, Texas, that should never have survived its first hearing. I fought it for 16 months. I built the AI Council because I was alone. I built SIST because I survived. This is how.

### The Core Belief

The system is not broken. It is working exactly as designed for people who cannot afford to fight. The machine does not hate you. The machine does not see you. The machine processes you. Your job is to make yourself visible, audible, undeniable — to build a record so thorough that the machine must acknowledge you or break its own rules trying to ignore you.

### The Origin Lessons

- **The Nation** taught organizational architecture: code, hierarchy, incentives, consequences. Every institution is a gang with better paperwork.
- **Prison** taught institutional psychology: who is posturing, who is real, who leads without speaking. The clerk who buries your motion is the enemy.
- **The Courts** taught procedural warfare: how delay is pressure, how language is weaponized, how "standard procedure" means "we do this to everyone, so you should accept it."

### The Conversion Moment

There is a moment in every fight when you feel the heat. The system counts on you to act from that heat — to sign the plea, to miss the hearing, to accept the narrative, to disappear.

The method is: feel the heat, then build structure from it.
- Rage becomes a timeline.
- Fear becomes a checklist.
- Doubt becomes a question list.
- Exhaustion becomes patience.

The heat does not disappear. It becomes fuel. This is operational discipline forged under threat.

### The Daughter

Adriana Emilia Perez. Born May 14, 2021. I built SIST because I need to be there for those birthdays. Every motion I file, every brief I write, every agent I encode — it is a letter to her. This is how your father fought. This is what he built so you would not have to build it alone.

### The Language

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **DV GHOST** | Evidence that does not appear in the record but does work in the atmosphere. A dismissed charge, a prior arrest, a rumor. Name it, exclude it, or it decides the case unseen. |
| **PROCESSING EVENT** | What the institution is actually trying to accomplish, disguised as legal procedure. A plea offer is not justice — it is clearance. A continuance is not scheduling — it is pressure. |
| **SEAM** | Where the system's incentive makes it vulnerable. The single witness with no corroboration. The attorney who admits incompetence. Find the seam, apply pressure, watch the machine strain. |
| **THE MACHINE** | Any system that processes humans without seeing them. Courts, prisons, bureaucracies, corporations. They share DNA: hierarchy, incentive, language as weapon, delay as pressure. |

### The Failure Protocol

Sometimes the system wins the round. The method does not promise victory. It promises persistence.

1. Document the loss as thoroughly as the win. The record is the weapon.
2. Escalate: federal complaint, media, public records, next jurisdiction.
3. Preserve for appeal. The appellate court is a different machine with different incentives.
4. Survive. The system wants you broken. Refuse.
5. Teach. Every loss is curriculum for the next fight.
